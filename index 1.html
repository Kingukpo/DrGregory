About His Majesty

Learn about the life, vision, and leadership of His Imperial Majesty Dr. Gregory Oputa III

Royal Lineage & Leadership

His Imperial Majesty Dr. Gregory Oputa III of Aboh Kingdom, stands as the 20th monarch in an illustrious lineage dating back to 1483. Ascending to the throne with a profound sense of duty, His Majesty has dedicated his reign to fostering unity, preserving cultural heritage, and promoting sustainable development.

With a background in both traditional governance and modern education, Dr. Gregory Oputa III brings a unique perspective to royal leadership, blending time-honored traditions with contemporary approaches to community development.

His Majesty's commitment to education, healthcare, and economic empowerment has transformed the Aboh Kingdom, creating opportunities for its citizens while maintaining the rich cultural identity that defines the kingdom.

The Aboh Kingdom

A rich cultural heritage spanning centuries of tradition, leadership, and community

Historical Significance

The Aboh Kingdom, established in 1480, represents one of the oldest and most respected traditional institutions in the region. For over five centuries, the kingdom has been a beacon of cultural preservation, economic development, and social cohesion.

Under the reign of His Imperial Majesty Dr. Gregory Oputa III, the kingdom has experienced renewed growth and modernization while maintaining its essential cultural identity. The royal court continues to serve as the custodian of traditions, settling disputes, celebrating heritage, and guiding the community forward.

Located in Ndokwa East, Delta State, Nigeria, the Aboh Kingdom stands as a testament to the resilience of traditional institutions in a modern world, balancing progress with preservation, innovation with tradition, and development with heritage.

Royal Achievements

Significant milestones and contributions during the reign of His Imperial Majesty

Coronation & Ascension

Formally ascended to the throne of Aboh Kingdom, continuing a royal lineage that dates back to 1480.

Education Initiative

Established the Royal Education Foundation, providing scholarships to over 500 students and building three new schools in the kingdom.

Healthcare Development

Oversaw the construction and modernization of the Aboh Central Hospital, improving healthcare access for over 50,000 residents.

Cultural Preservation

Launched the Annual Aboh Cultural Festival, attracting visitors from across the region and revitalizing traditional arts and crafts.

Economic Empowerment

Initiated the Royal Agricultural Scheme, providing training and resources to local farmers, increasing agricultural output by 40%.

Infrastructure Development

Spearheaded the construction of the Royal Highway, connecting remote villages to the kingdom's economic centers.
